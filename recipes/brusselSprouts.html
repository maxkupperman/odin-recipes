Brussel Sprouts

Description

Some people hate brussel sprouts. They've hated them since they were kids and they've never bothered to change their mind. Those people are wrong. Brussel sprouts, when cooked right, absolutely whip. I have a bad habit of calling them "Brussels Sprouts" because I have a false association between the name and the city of Brussels, which does have that extra S. You may see me make that typo in this very recipe!

This recipe works for any amount of brussel sprouts, provided you have a pan large enough to fit them on. Scale up the salt, pepper, and olive oil as needed. I assume you are an adult that can figure out how much salt, pepper, and olive oil you need to roast some vegetables in an oven, but do make sure you add enough olive oil to completely coat the brussel sprouts. Otherwise some sprouts will come out flavorless and nasty.

Ingredients

  • Brussel sprouts, stems cut off and chopped in half.
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350F. Line a baking tray with aluminum foil.
  2. In a bowl, add brussel sprouts, salt, pepper, and olive oil. Stir to combine until all the sprouts are coated with olive oil.
  3. Add sprouts to the tray. For best results, place each sprout with its flat, cross-sectioned face down on the tray.
  4. Cook in the oven for 50 minutes or until the sprouts are golden brown. Serve.
